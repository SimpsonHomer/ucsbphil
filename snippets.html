Philosophy 1
|
 Introduction to Philosophy
|
 BUCHN 1910 (MWF 11:00 - 11:50am + Discussion)
This course will give you an introduction to problems and arguments in modern philosophy of mind, theory of knowledge and metaphysics. In philosophy of mind, we will discuss questions like: What is the nature of the mind? Can the mind exist independently of the body? Or is our consciousness simply a physical thing, just like the body? Can any kind of physical thing then be said to have consciuosness, as long as it works like our brain does? In epistemology (theory of knowledge) we will discuss whether we can have knowledge of material things, or whether skepticism about the external world is impossible to refute. When do we accept that we know something? Do we know anything at all? Metaphysics covers discussions about what exists or does not exist. Does God exist? What are the fallacies in arguments for God's existence? Do we have free will? How can we have free will if we accept that the laws of nature are true and therefore determines our behavior as physical beings? How can we have moral responsibility if we are physically determined to behave like we do? This course will teach you how to approach a problem philosophically, that is, to structure a debate in terms of arguments and to critically assess those arguments. We will work with both a text book and with original literature.

Required Text(s)
Appiah, Thinking It Through: An Introduction to Contemporary Philosophy
