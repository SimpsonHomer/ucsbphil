Prerequisites: There are no prerequisites for this course

Course Goals and Objectives: By the end of the course, you should be able to:
Determine the logical structure of arguments, assess whether or not an argument is good or bad, construct proofs of conclusions from given premises and identify fallacious argument structures

Course Requirements and Grading:
Homework: 10% of your grade
Midterm 1: 25% of your grade
Midterm 2: 25% of your grade
Final: 40 % of your grade
All written tests must be completed in order to pass the course.

Homework: This is a course which requires you to acquire and hone skills. The homework is what will help you develop the skills without which you will not be able to take the tests. Homework assignments are distributed on Fridays and due one week later and will be graded for completeness.

Late homework: If your homework is turned in late without permission from your TA, the grade will be adjusted downwards by 1/3 grade for each day the homework is late (i.e., from A to A-, from A- to B+ etc.).

Attendance and Section Participation: Doing the homework and understanding why the solution was what is was, is absolutely crucial to do well in this course. Whereas attendance is not mandatory, it is strongly recommended in order to grasp the material.

Make-up Midterm/Final: In special circumstances this can be arranged. Please talk to your TA or me.

DSP: Accommodations will be made for DSP students. Please talk to me if you require assistance.

Plagiarism/Cheating: It is mandatory for instructors to report incidents of academic dishonesty. It is university policy to report all acts of academic dishonesty and that it is no longer up to the instructors whether or not to report such incidents. Plagiarism and cheating is antithetical to the purposes of the university and immature. The consequences of a report of plagiarism/cheating is severe and may affect your future academic and professional career.
