Fall 2010 Events

  • Friday, December 3rd: Sonny Elizondo (UCSB) will give a talk titled "More Than a Feeling: Kant on the Pleasure of Moral Activity".
  • Friday, September 10th: Anja Karnein (Goethe-University Frankfurt) will give a talk titled "The Moral Status of Future Persons".
  • Wednesday, October 6th-8th: Hartry Field (New York University) will be in the department as a Steven Humphrey Distinguished Visitor.  He will give a pair of lectures jointly titled "How, if at all, can we rationally revise our logic and inductive methods?" Lecture I ("The problem") will be on Wednesday, October 6th. Lecture II ("The solution") will be on Friday, October 8th.
  • Friday, October 15th: Teresa Robertson (University of Kansas) will give a talk titled "A Puzzle About Kinds".
  • Friday, November 5th: Mark Schroeder (University of Southern California) "Attitudes and Epistemics" in SH 5617 at 4pm.

*Update on administrative changes for Philosophy*

The staff supporting the Humanities Departments and Programs of Philosophy, English, Linguistics, ESL, and Writing in South Hall will be relocating on the days of September 9th through September 14th as part of an administrative restructuring. During this time, advising and academic services for the associated departments may be unavailable. After the 14th, support services will be available at the new Humanities South Administrative Support Center:

Student Services, South Hall 3432 x7488 (Undergraduate/Graduate Advising)
Academic Services, South Hall 3431 x7489 (Personnel, Financial, ESL Program)

Prior department/program offices will close (as administrative centers) over the course of this move. Phone and E-mail contact information for staff will also be changing. Please visit the following link after the move for up-to-date Humanities South Administrative Support Center Contact Info. Thank you for your patience during this transitional period!
