Research Abstract

I graduated from Dartmouth College in 2004 with an A.B. in Philosophy. My work as an undergraduate included examinations of Aristotle’s De Interpretatione Book IX, Kant’s Critique of Pure Reason and Maimonides’ Guide for the Perplexed. While studying at Dartmouth, I published a paper titled “An Examination of the Philosophical Implications of Computer Assisted Mathematical Proofs” in Aporia: The Undergraduate Journal of Philosophy at Dartmouth College.

I earned a J.D. from the Duke University School of Law in 2007 and then practiced corporate and securities law in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania through 2011. The focus of my practice was mergers and acquisitions, securities offerings, private equity fund formation and corporate governance. As an attorney, I published several articles in the Pepper Hamilton Corporate and Securities Law Update.

My research is currently focused on the ethics of targeted killings and Maimonides’ Guide for the Perplexed.

Personal
