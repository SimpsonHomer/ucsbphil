Research Abstract

I am a Californian, born and raised. My interest in philosophy began as a child through theological debates — which at this age includes hand-waving and foot-stomping — with my father. I received a B.A. in Philosophy and Psychology from UCLA, then a M.T.S. in Ethics, Philosophy, and Theology from Boston University.

Currently, I am researching Adam Smith, and the nature and authority of conscience. I am exploring the connections between conscience, impartiality, moral knowledge, and virtues. On the side, I am also fascinated by psychopaths, who are often described as ‘lacking a conscience’.

Personal

Though graduate school takes up most of my life, I do find time on the weekends to assist low-income students in the greater Los Angeles area. I love life in the city, especially within the arts (except musicals). Though I appreciate all that trees have to offer, I find (most) trees aesthetically displeasing.
