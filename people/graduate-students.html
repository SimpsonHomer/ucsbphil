Not all have applied widely for teaching positions in philosophy. Three never looked for teaching jobs at all --- they left the field after receiving their degrees. That leaves twenty-two students with Ph.D.s who have applied widely for teaching positions over the past decade. Eighteen of these have found tenure track jobs. The other four currently have temporary jobs. Of the eighteen who found tenure track jobs, nine found these jobs their first time on the job market. The other nine spent a year or two (in one case, three years) on temporary jobs first. Of the four who are currently on temporary jobs, three received their degrees very recently --- since spring 2005. The fourth got married while on a temporary job and is no longer searching outside of that geographical area. Here are the schools at which students have gotten tenure track jobs: - State University of New York (SUNY) at Buffalo - University of California, Irvine - California State University, Northridge - California State University, Sacramento - Humboldt State University - San Jose State University - Arizona State University - Western Michigan University - Weber State University - East Carolina State University - Georgia Southern University - Southern Illinois University at Edwardsville - Hollins University - Winthrop University - University of Tennessee at Chattanooga - Wright State University - The American University of Sharjah, UAE - University of Stirling - University of Glasgow - Spring Hill College, Alabama
